🤔 Deep-Think Prompts

💭
If you could reverse time, would entropy decrease?
This question reveals that entropy is fundamentally linked to time's arrow. In theory, microscopic physics is time-reversible, but statistically, the universe overwhelmingly favors states with higher entropy. Reversing time would reverse entropy locally, but this becomes exponentially improbable as system size increases.
🌱
A living organism seems to decrease entropy locally. Does this violate the 2nd Law?
No! Living organisms are open systems. While they create order internally (decreasing local entropy), they increase entropy in their environment even more through heat dissipation, waste, and metabolic processes. The net entropy of the universe (system + surroundings) always increases.
⚡
Why can't we build a perpetual motion machine if energy is conserved?
Energy conservation (1st Law) doesn't guarantee energy quality. The 2nd Law tells us that with every energy transformation, some energy becomes unavailable for work (increases as heat, disorder). A perpetual motion machine would need 100% efficiency forever, which entropy makes impossible.

📐 Algebra Fundamentals

Topic 1: Variables & Expressions

Algebra is a branch of mathematics that uses letters (variables) to represent unknown numbers. It helps us solve problems by setting up equations.

  • Variable: A letter representing an unknown number (e.g., x, y, a)
  • Expression: A combination of numbers, variables, and operations (e.g., 2x + 5)
  • Coefficient: The number multiplying a variable (e.g., in 3x, 3 is the coefficient)
  • Constant: A fixed number with no variable (e.g., 5 in 2x + 5)

Example: Simplify 2x + 3x - 5

= 5x - 5

Topic 2: Solving Linear Equations

A linear equation has variables with exponent 1. We solve by isolating the variable on one side.

  • Goal: Get the variable alone on one side
  • Rules: Whatever operation you do to one side, do to the other
  • Order: Subtract/Add constants first, then divide/multiply by coefficients

Example: Solve 2x + 5 = 13

2x + 5 - 5 = 13 - 5 (subtract 5)

2x = 8 (divide by 2)

x = 4

Topic 3: Polynomials & Factoring

Polynomials are expressions with multiple terms. Factoring breaks them into simpler parts.

  • Monomial: Single term (e.g., 5x)
  • Binomial: Two terms (e.g., x + 5)
  • Trinomial: Three terms (e.g., x² + 2x + 1)
  • Factoring: Writing as a product (e.g., (x+1)(x+1))

Example: Factor x² + 5x + 6

= (x + 2)(x + 3)

📏 Geometry Essentials

Topic 1: Points, Lines & Angles

Geometry is the study of shapes and space. It begins with basic building blocks.

  • Point: A location in space with no dimension
  • Line: Extends infinitely in both directions
  • Angle: Formed by two rays sharing an endpoint (measured in degrees)
  • Types: Acute (< 90°), Right (= 90°), Obtuse (> 90°), Straight (= 180°)

Topic 2: Triangles

A triangle is a polygon with 3 sides and 3 angles. The sum of angles = 180°.

  • Equilateral: All sides equal, all angles 60°
  • Isosceles: Two equal sides, two equal angles
  • Scalene: All sides different, all angles different
  • Area Formula: A = ½ × base × height

Topic 3: Circles & Volume

Circles are round shapes with all points equidistant from center. 3D solids have volume.

  • Circle Area: A = πr²
  • Circle Circumference: C = 2πr
  • Sphere Volume: V = ⁴⁄₃πr³
  • Cube Volume: V = s³

📊 Trigonometry

Topic 1: Trigonometric Ratios

Trigonometry studies relationships between angles and sides in triangles.

  • Sine (sin): Opposite / Hypotenuse
  • Cosine (cos): Adjacent / Hypotenuse
  • Tangent (tan): Opposite / Adjacent
  • Mnemonic: SOH-CAH-TOA

Topic 2: Special Angles

Common angles have known trigonometric values.

sin(30°) = 0.5, cos(30°) = √3/2, tan(30°) = 1/√3

sin(45°) = √2/2, cos(45°) = √2/2, tan(45°) = 1

sin(60°) = √3/2, cos(60°) = 0.5, tan(60°) = √3

Topic 3: Wave Functions

Sine and cosine create periodic waves used in physics and engineering.

  • Amplitude: Height of the wave
  • Period: Time for one complete cycle
  • Phase: Horizontal shift of the wave
  • Frequency: Number of cycles per unit time

∫ Calculus Basics

Topic 1: Limits & Continuity

A limit describes what value a function approaches as input gets close to some value.

  • Limit: Value that f(x) approaches as x approaches a number
  • Continuity: Function with no breaks or jumps
  • Notation: lim(x→a) f(x) = L

Topic 2: Derivatives

The derivative measures how fast a function changes at any point (the slope).

  • Derivative: Rate of change of a function
  • Notation: f'(x) or df/dx
  • Power Rule: d/dx(xⁿ) = n·xⁿ⁻¹
  • Applications: Finding max/min, velocity, acceleration

Topic 3: Integrals

Integration is the reverse of differentiation. It finds the area under a curve.

  • Indefinite Integral: ∫f(x)dx = F(x) + C
  • Definite Integral: ∫[a,b] f(x)dx = Area under curve
  • Power Rule: ∫xⁿ dx = xⁿ⁺¹/(n+1) + C
  • Applications: Area, volume, work, probability

⚛️ Physics Principles

Topic 1: Newton's Laws of Motion

Newton's laws describe how objects move in response to forces.

  • 1st Law: Object at rest stays at rest unless acted upon by force
  • 2nd Law: F = ma (Force = mass × acceleration)
  • 3rd Law: For every action, there's an equal opposite reaction

Topic 2: Energy & Work

Energy is the capacity to do work. It comes in many forms.

  • Kinetic Energy: KE = ½mv² (energy of motion)
  • Potential Energy: PE = mgh (stored energy)
  • Work: W = F × d (force times distance)
  • Conservation: Total energy stays constant (no friction)

Topic 3: Waves & Oscillations

Waves transport energy through space without moving matter.

  • Wavelength (λ): Distance between wave crests
  • Frequency (f): Number of waves per second
  • Wave Speed: v = f × λ
  • Types: Transverse (light) & Longitudinal (sound)

🧪 Chemistry Foundations

Topic 1: Atoms & Elements

All matter is made of atoms. Elements are pure substances with one type of atom.

  • Atom: Smallest unit of element (protons, neutrons, electrons)
  • Atomic Number: Number of protons (identifies the element)
  • Mass Number: Protons + Neutrons
  • Electron Configuration: How electrons arrange in shells

Topic 2: Chemical Bonds

Atoms bond together to form molecules and compounds.

  • Ionic Bond: Transfer of electrons between atoms
  • Covalent Bond: Sharing of electrons between atoms
  • Metallic Bond: Sea of electrons in metals
  • Valence Electrons: Outermost electrons involved in bonding

Topic 3: Chemical Reactions

Chemical reactions rearrange atoms to form new substances.

  • Reactants: Starting substances
  • Products: Resulting substances
  • Balanced Equation: Equal atoms on both sides
  • Types: Synthesis, decomposition, combustion, acid-base
